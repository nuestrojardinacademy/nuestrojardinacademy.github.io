Our Philosophy

Nuestro Jardín Academy believes in a well-rounded balanced educational approach to early childhood learning. We selected the best of many early childhood program philosophies, such as Reggio Emilia, Montessori, Waldorf, and Play Based, and apply them to our teaching practices.

With these theories and students’ learning styles in mind, the teachers at Nuestro Jardín use diverse teaching methodologies, integrating direct, inquiry-based instruction, and cooperative learning into everyday lessons. In addition, all activities throughout the day are geared to our diverse learners, and we make sure that all students’ learning styles are met (http://www.tecweb.org/styles/gardner.html).

90:10 Immersion Model

The 90:10 immersion model used at Nuestro Jardín immerses the student in Spanish 90% of the day. Extensive research has demonstrated that students who go through a 90:10 program consistently develop high levels of comprehension and reading and writing proficiency in their second language. They also display confidence when using it.

Research has also shown that students in a 90:10 immersion program develop higher levels of bilingualism, biliteracy, and cognitive skills when compared with English-only proficient students participating in other educational programs. Another benefit of students being in a dual immersion program is that they will develop very positive attitudes about students of other language and cultural backgrounds, and positive attitudes toward themselves as learners. Consult the Center for Applied Linguistics for specific research studies.

Nurturing the Whole Child

The whole child approach taken by Nuestro Jardín Academy ensures that each student is healthy, safe, engaged, supported, and challenged by addressing students' needs through the shared responsibility of students, families, and the community.

Our Curriculum

Standards set the basis for what children should know and be able to do based on their age and developmental level. Nuestro Jardín’s standards have been carefully created and are backed by solid research in early childhood and second language learning. Our curriculum was created to develop the whole child and allow teachers to collaborate and teach to a common goal. Our thematic approach adopts a monthly theme that is culturally relevant and personally emerges from students to enrich Nuestro Jardín Academy’s curriculum.

Fostering Cultural Awareness

At Nuestro Jardín, we believe in the importance of raising cultural awareness in students, teaching diversity, and growing responsible global citizens. We help children understand, embrace, and respect differences and similarities among those in our community. To this end, we integrate culturally-rich activities in our every-day curriculum, and two Fridays per month, we celebrate Día del Patrimonio (Cultural Heritage Day) by immersing ourselves in a culture from a country or region around the world.

We also help children understand who they are in the context of their race, ethnic group, culture, language and familial history. Through dramatic and role-play activities, students are encouraged to express themselves within the context of their identity.

We take pride in our diverse community and we encourage our families to share their culture and heritage in the classroom.

Guelaguetza Celebrations
The Guelaguetza, or Los lunes del cerro (Mondays on the Hill) is an annual indigenous cultural event in Mexico that takes place in the city of Oaxaca, capital of the state of Oaxaca, as well as in nearby villages. The celebration centers on traditional dancing in costume in groups, often gender-separated groups, as is traditional, and includes parades complete with indigenous walking bands, native food, and statewide artisanal crafts such as prehispanic-style textiles. Each costume (traje) and dance usually has a local indigenous historical and cultural meaning. Although the celebration is now an important tourist attraction, it also retains deep cultural importance for the peoples of the state and is important for the continuing survival of these cultures.

Events at Nuestro Jardín

Community Night - 2nd Wednesday of every other month
One of the core beliefs at Nuestro Jardín is to build a community, bringing together students, parents, and teachers. Community Night is a time for all families to get together and get to know one another. It is also a time for parents and teachers to develop stronger partnerships and to discuss the latest teaching and parenting techniques used at Nuestro Jardín. After a family potluck, a parent meeting will follow.

Mom’s Night Out (MNO) - 1st Wednesday of each month
This is the time for all moms at Nuestro Jardín to get out and get some ‘me time’ with other moms. At the beginning of each school year, the group will decide on the events for each month (dinner, movie, dancing, working out, book club, etc.)

Dad’s Night Out (DNO) - 3rd Wednesday of each month
And dads could not be forgotten! They, too, will get some ‘me time’ with other Nuestro Jardín’s dads. Similarly to MNO, dads will decide on the events for each month at the beginning of each school year (dinner, movie, working out, book club, game night, etc.)

Kids’ Night Out aka Parents’ Night Out - Last Friday of every month (except for Nov. & Dec.)
Of course children would love to have some fun while their parents get a child-free night out. A movie, crafts, and stories! We can do all these and more during this event. Open to toilet-trained children 3-8 years old. Children are welcome to wear their pajamas. Availability is limited.
