FULL AND PART TIME SPANISH PRESCHOOL TEACHER (NE Portland)

Open until filled

Nuestro Jardín Academy, a brand new Spanish immersion preschool in Portland, Oregon, is seeking full and part time Spanish-speaking preschool teachers to start up in late spring/beginning of summer 2016. Our preschool provides a high-quality, research-based program that fosters the acquisition of children's first and second language with a focus on developing the whole child. We believe in making this a unique language and cultural immersion experience for children, so we are looking for the perfect candidates to join our team. Our goal is for teachers to work in a fun, supportive, and collaborative environment. Teachers are one of the best assets to our program, so providing high-quality professional development in Spanish will help you become the best teacher you can be.

Job Description:
ESSENTIAL FUNCTIONS:

  • Responsible for the overall supervision, planning, and management of the Spanish immersion preschool class:
  • Create and manage a warm and challenging learning environment for students, incorporating appropriate activities which meet the needs of the group as well as those of individual children and families.
  • Provide and maintain a safe environment.
  • Be able to read, speak, and write with native-like fluency and proficiency in Spanish.

RESPONSIBILITIES:

  • Be available to substitute for other classes as needed.
  • Participate in professional development activities and complete continuing education requirements.
  • Adhere to personnel policies as outlined in the Employee Handbook.
  • Be responsible and oversee the accomplishment of routine tasks with regard to the health, safety, and well-being of children, and maintain the program in accordance with the requirements of the Oregon Child Care Division.
  • Assist in the maintenance of a safe, healthy, and orderly classroom environment.
  • Assist in designing and maintaining a warm, nurturing, enriched, and challenging learning environment.
  • Assist in creating, revising, and implementing lesson plans based on our Spanish immersion program’s goals and objectives.

ADDITIONAL EXPECTATIONS:

Program Participation and Team Member:

  • Arrive to work on time; punctually attend and actively participate in all required activities, meetings, and trainings.
  • Be a respectful, cooperative, and reliable team member and participant in program activities.
  • Project a professional work image
  • Be familiar with, and adhere to, position-related policies and procedures as well as the program’s overarching objectives.

Confidentiality:
 Respect and maintain confidentiality of information regarding enrolled children and families, personnel issues, and other program operations as appropriate.

Training:
 New teachers must satisfactorily complete a training program before summer camp sessions and/or the new school year start.

Commitment:
 A minimum of one year commitment is required. Both part-time and full-time new hires will be working under a contract.

Hours:
 Part-Time: 20-30 hours per week
Full-Time: 34-38 hours per week

MINIMUM QUALIFICATIONS:

  • Applicant must be bilingual AND be a native Spanish speaker or have native-like fluency/proficiency
  • Qualify under one of the following below:
    • AA Degree in early childhood education, elementary education, child development, or special education OR
    • At least two years of experience working with young children and families in a daycare/ preschool program. OR
    • Completion of 20 semester credits or 30 quarter credits of training at a college or university in Early childhood education, child development, elementary education, or special education AND at least one year of experience working with young children and families in a daycare/preschool program.
  • Enrollment in Oregon Central Background Registry (can be done right after accepting employment)
  • The following are also required for employment and must be obtained within 90 days after accepting the position.
    • Current First Aid/CPR card
    • Food Handlers Card
    • Valid Oregon Driver’s License

PREFERRED QUALIFICATIONS (not required but a plus):

Skills, expertise, or aptitude in:

  • the visual arts
  • the performing arts (dance, music, drama)
  • second language teaching

CALENDAR INFORMATION:

Nuestro Jardín Academy mostly follows the Portland Public Schools Calendar:

  • 2 weeks off in December
  • 1 week off in Spring
  • 8 observed holidays during the school year
  • 4 days of professional development/planning days before the school year begins
  • 5 days of professional development/planning days throughout the school year
Summer camp sessions:
  • Run for part of June, July, and part of August.
  • 4 weeks off during the summer.
  • 1 observed holiday in the summer

COMPENSATION & BENEFITS:

  • $11-$15 per hour depending on education and work experience
  • 7 weeks off in a year
  • One week of paid sick/personal days
  • Teacher planning built into schedule
  • Tuition discount for any children attending Nuestro Jardín Academy
After 90 days of full-time employment (34+ hours a week):
  • Medical benefits
  • Two weeks paid vacation during Winter and Spring breaks
